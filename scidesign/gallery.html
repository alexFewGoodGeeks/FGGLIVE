Design work for Helsinki University

  • Cover Picture: Targeted drug delivery to damaged organs

    Published in: Drug Delivery: Drug‐Loaded Multifunctional Nanoparticles Targetedto the Endocardial Layer of the Injured Heart Modulate Hypertrophic Signaling Volume 13, Issue 33

  • Publishing Date: 01 September 2017
  • Abstract

    Coronary heart disease is the main cause of death worldwide, the endocardium being the most vulnerable region for myocardial ischemia. In article number 1701276, by Mónica P. A. Ferreira, Heikki Ruskoaho, Anu J. Airaksinen, Hélder A. Santos, and co-workers, the development of a multifunctional nanoparticle with cardioprotective potential is reported, which selectively targets the endocardial layer of the left ventricle upon intravenous injection and delivers adrug molecule for modulating the hypertrophic signaling.

Design work for Helsinki University

  • Cover Picture: Nanoparticle Assisted Antibody Growth and Cancer Treatment

    Published in: Drug Delivery: Drug‐Loaded Multifunctional Nanoparticles Targetedto the Endocardial Layer of the Injured Heart Modulate Hypertrophic Signaling Volume 27, Issue 42

  • Publishing Date: 08 November 2017
  • Abstract

    The immune system can be intelligently programmed to fight cancer. In article number 1703303 by Mohammad-Ali Shahbazi, Bruno Sarmento, Hélder A. Santos, and co-workers, spermine-modified acetalated dextran nanoparticles are co-loaded with a MDM-2 inhibitor and a cytokine for chemo-immunotherapy. Immune cell recruitment to the cancer cells and its activation synergize the anticancer impact of the drug molecules.

Design work for Helsinki University

  • Cover published in the special issue celebrating science progress in Finland

    Published in: Special Issue: Materials Science in Finland Volume 30, Issue 24

  • Publishing Date: 08 June 2018
  • Abstract

    This special issue is dedicated to current research activities on materials science in Finland. The issue provides a collection of outstanding contributions from diverse research groups on the recent progress on silicon and silica nanomaterials, DNA nanotechnology, micro-/nanomotors, biomass-based nanostructures, nanocellulose, 2D layered materials, atomic-layer deposition, superhydrophobic surfaces, and microrobots, from University of Helsinki, Aalto University, VTT, University of Turku, Åbo Akademi University, Tampere University of Technology, and University of Eastern Finland.

Design work for Helsinki University

  • Cover Picture: Intestinal Walls Treatment with Nanoparticle Clusters

    Published in: Drug Delivery: pH and Reactive Oxygen Species-Sequential Responsive Nano-in-Micro Composite for Targeted Therapy of Inflammatory Bowel Disease Volume 28, Issue 50

  • Publishing Date: 10 December 2018
  • Abstract

    In article number 1806175, Nadia Passerini, Wei Li, Hélder A. Santos, and co-workers report a nano-in-micro composite for targeted therapy of inflammatory bowel disease via oral administration. The composite responds sequentially to pH and reactive oxygen species. The drug permeation through intestinal epithelium is also limited, providing a promising approach to limit unspecific absorption and systemic side effects.

Design work for Helsinki University

  • Cover Picture: Cancer Immunotherapy Using Nanovaccines Based on Porous Silicon

    Published in: Nanovaccines: Multistaged Nanovaccines Based on Porous Silicon@Acetalated Dextran@Cancer Cell Membrane for Cancer Immunotherapy Volume 29, Issue 7

  • Publishing Date: 13 February 2017
  • Abstract

    In article 1603239, Flavia Fontana, Hélder A. Santos, and co-workers develop an innovative cancer nanovaccine by merging the adjuvant properties of the nanoparticles produced by nanoprecipitation in a microfluidic glass capillary device with high reproducibility, together with a biomimetic source of antigens derived from cancer-cell membranes. The nanovectors induce maturation of antigen-presenting cells and secretion of pro-inflammatory cytokines in vitro.

Design work for Helsinki University

  • Cover Picture: Multidisciplinary approach to the advancement of cancer-specific nanomedicine

    Published in: Nanomedicine: Bridging the Knowledge of Different Worlds to Understand the Big Picture of Cancer Nanomedicines Volume 7, Issue 1

  • Publishing Date: 10 December 2018
  • Abstract

    Nanomedicine is emerging as effective therapeutic strategy for cancer therapy. In addition, cancer nanomedicine is at the interface of several diverse fields and this, taken together with the complexities of nanomedicine and cancer, requires a multidisciplinary effort to advance the understanding and development of cancer-specific nanomedicine in order to meet the current challenges in translating nanomedicine into the clinic. In article number 1700432 Vimalkumar Balasubramanian, Hélder A. Santos, and co-workers review nanomedicine for cancer therapy.

Design work for Helsinki University

  • Inside Cover Picture: Liver Regeneration with Multifunctional Nanohybrids

    Published in: Nanohybrids: Multifunctional Nanohybrid Based on Porous Silicon Nanoparticles, Gold Nanoparticles, and Acetalated Dextran for Liver Regeneration and Acute Liver Failure Theranostics

  • Publishing Date: 08 June 2018
  • Abstract

    A nanohybrid based on porous silicon (PSi) nanoparticles, gold nanoparticles, and acetalated dextran is designed for acute liver failure (ALF) reversal and CT-imaging-facilitated ALF indication. In article number 1703393, Hongbo Zhang, Dawang Zhou, Xianming Deng, Hélder A. Santos, and co-workers develop a PSi-based nanohybrid composite for liver regeneration and ALF treatment. This system is used to identify pathological changes in the tissues and selectively deliver drugs to these sites, facilitating imaging in vivo, and is thus a great potential theranostic platform for ALF.
